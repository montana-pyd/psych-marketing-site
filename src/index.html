Who we are

The Montana Positive Youth Development Program is a training and community engagement program led by me, Dr. Benjamin Oosterhoff. Dr. Oosterhoff is an Assistant Professor in the Psychology Department at Montana State University who specializes in positive youth development and adolescent civic engagement. The Montana positive youth development program is also composed of a talented team of undergraduate research assistants all with an interest in positive psychology, community engagement, and adolescent development.

What we do

The Positive Youth Development Research Program at Montana State University seeks to enhance the health, well-being, and education of young people by advancing research, training, and application of knowledge concerning positive development. We are committed to improving the lives of youth and their communities by proving opportunities for young people to connect with researchers, educators, and policy makers, while simultaneously identifying and promoting the strengths of youth as well as their schools and communities.

What we value

At the Positive Youth Development program, we value the strengths and abilities of all youth, regardless of background or past experiences. We celebrate the diversity of individuals, families, communities, and cultures. We value the scientific process and building credible knowledge in manner that transparent and authentic.

Research

We believe that it is our responsibility to the current and future generations of young people to develop scientifically sound knowledge concerning how to best promote their health, well-being, and education. Our research identifies how family, school, and community contexts can be used to support the positive youth development.

Training

We believe that all students have the ability to achieve personal growth and improve their community. We prepare students at multiple stages of training (secondary school, undergraduate, graduate) with the skills necessary to thrive as consumers, producers, and translators of scientific knowledge concerning positive youth development. This training not only teaches students how to create new knowledge, but also shows them how to share that knowledge with their community.

Engagement

We believe that the best way to having a lasting effect on youth is to become directly involved in their community. We provide parents, teachers, and school administrators with direct training in how to improve child and adolescent health, well-being, and education. Students in our program also create a monthly newsletter and infographs describing new research on positive youth development.
